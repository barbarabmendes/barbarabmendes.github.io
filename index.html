About

Welcome to my site on the world wide web. I am Bárbara, PhD in Tissue Engineering. I am 28 years-old and was born in Fátima, which is located right in centre region of Portugal.

Research

For nearly five years, I have been researching and engineering intrinsically bioactive systems as mimics of the native extracellular matrix that find multiple research and clinical applications in regenerative medicine and tissue engineering field.

Education

PhD in Tissue Engineering, Regenerative Medicine and Stem Cells/Universidade do Minho, Summa cum laude
Sep 2015 - Jul 2020

In September of 2015 she was accepted to the PhD Program in Tissue Engineering, Regenerative Medicine & Stem Cells of the University of Minho (Portugal) and awarded the PhD scholarship from Portuguese Science Foundation under the scope of this program. Since then she is conducting her PhD research at the 3B’s Research Group, I3Bs Institute of the University of Minho, under the supervision of Prof. Manuela Gomes and Dr. Rui Domingues, and in collaboration with the University of Pennsylvania (USA) under the supervision of Professor Jason Burdick.

Master in Biomedical Engineering/Universidade do Porto, 17 of 20
Sep 2012 - Oct 2014

My master thesis was developed under supervision of Professor Bruno Sarmento and Professor Domingos Ferreira entitled “Development and characterization of a co-culture two-dimensional blood-brain barrier for the study of nanoparticle permeation”.

Bachelor in Biomedical Sciences/Universidade da Beira Interior/15 of 20
Sep 2009 - July 2012

My report was focused on the development of nanoparticles to improve the drug efficiency delivery.

During my B.Sc., I had opportunity to work with Professor Carla S. P. Fonseca at CICS-UBI. Her group has been investigate the mechanisms underlying Parkinson's disease. This was my first contact with research experiments and primary cell culture studies.
